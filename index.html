JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is a Japanese manga series created by Hirohiko Araki. It first appeared in Shueisha's Weekly Shōnen Jump magazine in 1987 and continued there until 2004, before transitioning to the monthly seinen magazine Ultra Jump in 2005.

The series is structured into nine distinct story arcs, each centered on a new protagonist who carries the "JoJo" nickname.

Renowned as one of the most influential and best-selling manga worldwide, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has left a profound mark on global pop culture. Its impact extends far beyond traditional manga, inspiring references in various media, from music videos to films.

The series’ bold and avant-garde art style has influenced the world of fashion, with its designs appearing on runways and in collaborations with major brands. Additionally, its creative brilliance has earned it a place in art galleries, where exhibits showcase the manga's unique aesthetic and narrative innovation.
