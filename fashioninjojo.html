Media References in JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Fashion, Film and Art

Araki's passion for high fashion is another defining element of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure. Giving the character their unique look that makes them stand out more than others.
Characters are often depicted in striking, flamboyant outfits that draw heavily from contemporary fashion trends, movie characters and iconic designers.

Fashion

Araki has mentioned being influenced by designers like Versace and Moschino, known for their bold and often extravagant styles. This is reflected in the characters' elaborate costumes, intricate accessories, and dramatic poses, often reminiscent of fashion magazine spreads.

Along with his other beautiful forms of expression, Araki uses extravagant forms of fashion to express his character's personalities, social status, or even their Stand abilities. The evolution of character designs throughout the series also mirrors the changing fashion trends of the respective time periods in which each part is set Araki's distinctive art style is known for its dynamic poses, often referred to as "JoJo poses." These poses have also been inspired by many fashion icons, most notably vogue covers.

The series' connection to fashion extends beyond the manga itself. Araki's art books, such as JoJo A-Go! Go!, often showcases characters in high-fashion attire, he has even collaborated with brands like Gucci and his art has made appearances in SPUR magazine.
Araki's love and form of expression has left impact on the world even beyond his own series showing just how amazing artistic influence can be.

Art and Film

While music and fashion are the most prominent references, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure also draws inspiration from film and other art forms.

The series incorporates elements from various film genres, such as horror, action, and thriller, which contribute to the narrative's dynamic and suspenseful nature. Certain scenes and plotlines may even directly reference specific films or directors.
One of the series most distinct protagonists, Jotaro Kujo is said to be inspired by Hollywood actor, Clint Eastwood and a key plot device in multiple parts, the vampires were inspired by fright night and dracula.
Araki has also stated that he learned how to write up scenarios in the early parts by studying film director Alfred Hitchcock's movies and interviews.

Hirohiko Araki's extensive use of media references in JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is more than just a collection of Easter eggs. It's a fundamental aspect of the series' unique identity, enriching the narrative, character development, and visual style. By weaving together his passion for music, fashion, film, and art, Araki has created a truly multifaceted and culturally resonant work that continues to captivate audiences worldwide.
