Media References in JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Music

Music is a big foundation for the series and perhaps the most prominent and consistent form of media reference. Araki's use of music, primarily Western rock and pop music from the 1970s, 80s, and 90s is a fundamental aspect of the series' unique identity, enriching the narrative, character development, visual style and leaving long lasting, iconic names for the readers.
Many characters and Stands are directly named after musical artists, albums, and songs leaving a greater impact than the more minor characters.
This started from Phantom Blood, with the main antagonist Dio being named after Ronnie James Dio and his band DIO, Speedwagon was named after the rock band REO Speedwagon and the Zeppeli surname seems to be a tribute to the band Led Zeppelin Dire and Straizo who were also some of Jonathan Joestar's first allies are a direct reference to the English rock band Dire Straits. The theme continued throughout Battle Tendency, with the main antagonists the Pillar Men all named after iconic music figures. Staring with the hard rock band AC/DC, the pop duo Wham!, the American guitarist Santana, and the American rock band The Cars.

The introduction of stands.

With the introduction of stands in Stardust Crusaders, music started influencing artistic design and attack names with some of the casts stands being designed off albums and their abilities being references to lyrics. Like iggy who's name comes from pop singer Iggy Pop and his stand The Fool which is based off Iggy Pop's album The Idiot.
Similarly Muhammad Avdol, named after American singer Paula Abdul has an attack called Crossfire Hurricane, derived from the Rolling Stones song Jumpin'Jack Flash. It is also speculated among fans that his stand is based off the song The Magician's Birthday by Uriah Heep.
A more unique approach to the musical references was seen in Stone Ocean where Green Baby's stand Green Grass of Home is replicated the pose from the Nirvana album cover of Nevermind.
Albums, songs and artists for character names continues all the way to the current last part with some of the most popular ones being: DIO, Stone Free, KISS, Foo Fighters, Bohemian Rhapsody, C-moon, Sex Pistols, Sticky Finger, Aerosmith and more.

The alternate universe.

The Steel Ball Run universe continued the name and stand name thematic with a unique addition of naming horses after songs and bands.
Johnny's horse is named after the song and album Slow Dancer by Boz Scaggs, Diego's is named after the band Silver Bullet and Pocoloco's stand and horse are both named Hey Ya! which is a popular pop song by outkast.
Having more than just the stands named after musical themes shows just how much Araki is inspired by music.
